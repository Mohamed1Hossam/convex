2.1 MATHEMATICAL JUSTIFICATION OF CONVEXITY
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The objective function is composed of two parts:

1) Quadratic risk term: x^T Σ x
   • A quadratic form x^T Σ x is convex if and only if Σ is positive semidefinite (PSD).
   • From the eigenvalue analysis, the minimum eigenvalue of Σ is >= 0, confirming PSD.
   • Therefore, the risk term is convex.

2) Linear return term: -λ μ^T x
   • Linear (affine) functions are both convex and concave.
   • Subtracting a linear term does not change convexity.

Since a convex function plus an affine function is still convex, the total objective:
        f(x) = x^T Σ x - λ μ^T x
is convex.

The constraints (sum(x)=1, x>=0, x<=max_alloc) are linear, and linear constraints preserve convexity.
Therefore, the entire optimization problem is convex.
